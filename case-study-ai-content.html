System prompts are content design

When Instagram started building LLM-powered ad CTAs, I embedded myself in the work, rewrote the system prompts from scratch, and drove 3%+ CTR lifts at scale. Then I built the framework and playbook now used by content designers across Meta's AI work.

Instagram Business  ·  2026

Challenge

Instagram was building LLM-powered ad CTAs, but content design wasn't in the room where the prompts were written. Engineering owned the system prompts, and the outputs varied widely in quality. Without content design expertise inside the prompt itself, no amount of polish on the UI layer could fix what the model was producing.

The deeper problem was that nobody had a model for what content design looked like inside an LLM. The discipline had a clear role on screens. It had no defined role inside the prompt. If we were going to ship LLM-generated content at Meta's scale, that gap had to close fast.

Constraints
Discipline boundary

System prompt work lived inside engineering. Content design had to earn its way into the workflow, not be invited.

No precedent

No playbook existed for content design inside LLMs at Meta. The methodology had to be built while the work was happening.

Evaluation

There was no agreed-upon way to judge whether an LLM-generated CTA was good. Without a quality bar, we couldn't tell if changes were working.

Scale

Outputs would appear on billions of ad impressions. The quality threshold for shipping had to match Meta's scale, not a sandbox.

Skill gap

For the methodology to scale beyond me, other content designers had to learn prompting fast enough to lead their own AI projects.

Approach
Step 01
Align & Access

Got leadership buy-in to embed CD into the system prompt workflow, met with key POCs, and learned system prompting from Content Engineers across Meta.

→
Step 02
Audit & Rewrite

Identified patterns and gaps in the existing prompt. Rewrote it section by section, bringing CD expertise: the ingredients of an effective CTA, what to prioritize, and what to avoid.

→
Step 03
Test & Iterate

Partnered with Eng to test improvements in a sandbox. Developed quality evaluation criteria with PM, ran quality parties, and iterated until results were consistently stronger than baseline CTAs.

→
Step 04
Ship, Measure & Scale

Aligned on public test, kicked off, measured 3%+ CTR lift, then turned the methodology into Meta's first system prompt playbook for content designers.

Key insight

Whatever we end up being called, content design expertise is needed to generate high-quality outputs from LLMs.

What I'd do differently: Next time, I'll start documenting the methodology while doing the work, not after. Earlier playbooks would have accelerated adoption across other AI teams.
