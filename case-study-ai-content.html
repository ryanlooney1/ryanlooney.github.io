Overview
The challenge

System prompts — the instructions that shape how LLMs generate user-facing content — had no content design involvement. Engineers and PMs were writing the words that reached users, without a content design lens.

My role

As Instagram Business AI Lead, I learned system prompting from the ground up, inserted myself into the literal prompt-writing process, and built a playbook and evaluation criteria for XFN partners.

My involvement in LLM-powered CTAs drove significant wins in dwell time, clicks, and ad conversions — and established content design as a core discipline in Meta's AI product work.

The challenge

When Instagram began building LLM-powered features, the system prompts driving user-facing content were being written without content design input. I recognized this as both a risk and an opportunity. The words reaching users — generated at scale by AI — were being shaped entirely by engineers and PMs. Content design had a role to play, and nobody had claimed it yet.

I learned system prompting deeply: how instructions shape model behavior, how tone and constraints interact, how small wording decisions produce dramatically different outputs at scale. Then I put that knowledge to work.

Key insight

System prompts are content design. They define how a product thinks, speaks, and behaves — at a scale no human writer could match alone.

Approach
1. Inserted into the process

I joined the literal prompt-writing workflow for Instagram's LLM-powered features, contributing directly to the instructions shaping AI-generated content at scale.

2. Built a playbook

I documented what worked — prompt structures, tone frameworks, constraint patterns — and developed evaluation criteria that XFN partners could use to assess output quality independently.

3. Drove measurable outcomes

On LLM-powered CTAs specifically, my content design involvement directly improved output quality and drove significant gains in dwell time, clicks, and ad conversions.

Impact

Content design found its place in Meta's AI product work — not by waiting to be invited, but by showing up and proving its value.

  • Built Meta's first content design playbook for system prompt writing
  • Created evaluation criteria adopted by XFN partners across AI product teams
  • LLM-powered CTAs delivered significant wins in dwell time, clicks, and ad conversions
  • Established content design as a core discipline in Instagram's AI product development
What this project shows

The best time to establish content design in AI is before anyone asks for it

This work reflects how I approach new territory: learn it deeply, find where the leverage is, and build the frameworks that let others follow. I didn't wait for a seat at the table — I showed up with a system prompt and a playbook.
