Improving the default call to action

Finding an ownership gap in Meta's ad infrastructure — and turning it into a testing program that changed the default CTA for billions of impressions.

Overview
The challenge

Nobody at Meta formally owned or managed the list of ad CTAs available to advertisers — despite billions of daily impressions. "Learn More" was the dominant default not because it was best, but because no one had revisited it.

My role

I identified the ownership gap, established accountability, led a testing program of 1,000+ variants, and aligned leadership across Meta’s monetization teams around a new direction.

The outcome

Meta launched its first new default ad CTA. Dynamic CTAs gave Instagram advertisers more relevant options and delivered measurable improvements in advertiser performance.

The challenge

Ad CTAs touch every advertiser on Instagram and Facebook — billions of impressions daily. But when I started investigating, I discovered that no team at Meta formally owned the list of available CTAs. "Learn More" had become the default not by design, but by neglect. It was everywhere because no one had looked hard enough to change it.

I recognized this as a significant untapped opportunity. If even a small improvement to the default CTA increased advertiser performance, the impact at Meta's scale would be enormous.

Key insight

The biggest content design opportunities are often hiding in plain sight — in the elements so familiar that nobody thinks to question them.

Approach
1. Found who owned it (nobody)

I mapped ownership across monetization, product, and content teams and confirmed the gap was real. Establishing accountability was the first step to making change possible.

2. Built and ran a testing program

I created and tested 1,000+ CTA variants across Instagram ad surfaces to identify what language actually drove better advertiser outcomes at scale.

3. Aligned leadership and launched

I brought findings to leadership across Meta's monetization teams, built the case for a new default, and helped drive the first new default ad CTA to launch.

Impact

By finding the gap nobody else had looked for, this project changed a piece of Meta's ad infrastructure that touched every advertiser on the platform.

  • Delivered Meta's first new default ad CTA
  • Launched dynamic CTAs giving Instagram advertisers more relevant, specific options
  • Generated measurable improvements in advertiser dwell time, clicks, and performance
  • Established ownership and process for ongoing CTA development across Meta
What this project shows

Small interface moments can have outsized impact

This project highlights how I approach seemingly simple UX problems: identify the hidden complexity, clarify the user decision, and create a content solution that improves both the experience in front of the user and the system behind it.
