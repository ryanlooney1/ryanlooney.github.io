Simplifying Meta's global ad label

Changing “Sponsored” to “Ad” across Meta apps to improve clarity, reduce visual clutter, and unlock meaningful revenue.

Role: Content Design Lead Scope: Cross-Meta Focus: Monetization UX
Hero screenshot of the updated ad label experience
Overview
The problem

The legacy “Sponsored” label added friction, took up more space, and had never been systematically re-evaluated across Meta surfaces.

The opportunity

A simpler label could improve clarity, support cleaner UI patterns, and create more flexibility across product experiences.

My role

I led the cross-functional content strategy, aligned stakeholders, and helped drive the change from concept through rollout planning.

The outcome

The shift to “Ad” created a clearer, more scalable system that improved the user experience while supporting business goals.

The challenge

“Sponsored” had been part of Meta’s ad experience since 2006. Over time, it became a default pattern rather than an intentionally optimized one. I identified an opportunity to revisit the label through a modern content design lens: make it faster to parse, easier to scale, and better aligned with how people actually scan interfaces.

Key insight

This was not just a copy change. A single-word decision affected comprehension, trust, layout flexibility, cross-app consistency, and monetization at global scale.

Approach
1. Reframed the problem

I positioned the work as both a clarity improvement and a systems-level opportunity, helping teams see the change as more than a text tweak.

2. Built alignment

I brought together partners across content design, product, research, legal, and other cross-functional teams to evaluate risks and opportunities.

3. Scaled decision-making

I used AI tools to summarize inputs, track open questions, and help teams move through complex discussions more efficiently.

4. Planned for rollout

I helped shape a scalable path that could work across Meta apps and support consistent implementation over time.
