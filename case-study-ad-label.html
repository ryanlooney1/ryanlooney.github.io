Simplifying Meta's global ad label

Changing “Sponsored” to “Ad” across Meta apps to improve clarity, reduce visual clutter, and unlock meaningful revenue.

Role: Content Design Lead Scope: Cross-Meta Focus: Monetization UX

Overview
The challenge

The legacy “Sponsored” label had gone largely unquestioned for years, even though it added friction, consumed more space, and was never systematically re-evaluated across Meta surfaces.

My role

I led the content strategy, aligned cross-functional stakeholders, and helped drive the change from identifying the opportunity through rollout planning.

The outcome

The shift to “Ad” created a clearer, more scalable pattern that improved the user experience while supporting broader business goals.

The challenge

“Sponsored” had been part of Meta’s ad experience since 2006. Over time, it became a default pattern rather than an intentionally optimized one. I identified an opportunity to revisit the label through a modern content design lens: make it faster to parse, easier to scale, and better aligned with how people actually scan interfaces.

Key insight

This was not just a copy change. A single-word decision affected comprehension, trust, layout flexibility, cross-app consistency, and monetization at global scale.

Approach
1. Reframed the opportunity

I positioned the work as both a clarity improvement and a systems-level opportunity, helping teams see the change as more than a small text tweak.

2. Built cross-functional alignment

I brought together partners across content design, product, research, legal, and adjacent teams to evaluate tradeoffs, risks, and rollout considerations.

3. Planned for scale

I helped shape a path that could work across Meta apps, support consistent implementation, and create a stronger foundation for future monetization improvements.

Impact

By changing a single high-volume label, this work improved clarity at scale while creating more flexibility for product and monetization teams.

  • Simplified a long-standing label across Meta ad experiences
  • Reduced visual clutter and improved scanability in the interface
  • Created more scalable, space-efficient patterns that supported revenue growth
What this project shows

Content design can drive system-level change
